STANDING UP FOR VICTIMS OF

Police Brutality

Standing By You After A Violent Encounter with Police

If you or a family member is looking for a lawyer to represent you in a case involving police brutality in the Austin area, we’re here to help. We represent victims of police brutality and their families in cases involving police shootings and excessive use of force by police.

Police violence is a serious problem in the United States, and one that doesn’t affect all Americans equally. The rate of fatal shootings by police differs greatly based on ethnicity, highlighting a culture of racism in law enforcement. Between 2015 and October 2021, the rate of fatal police shootings was 37 for every million Black Americans and 28 for every million Hispanic Americans. In contrast, the rate for White Americans was significantly lower at 15 fatal police shootings per million white people (source Statista Research Department). 

In recent years, there has been an increase in international awareness of police violence and police racism spurred on by several high-profile cases such as Trayvon Martin, Breonna Taylor, and George Floyd. Tragically, despite the publicity surrounding these cases, the Black Lives Matter movement, and the protests that took place all over the world, there is still an upward trend in fatal police shootings in our country. 

Legal Representation for Victims of Police Violence

Hendler Flores Law is committed to holding police officers accountable for unnecessary violence. Our firm represents several victims and their families in Austin in cases involving police brutality. We represent Brenda Ramos in her fight for justice for her son Mike Ramos, who was fatally shot by police in the back of the head through his car window. Our firm also represents several victims of police violence who were injured at a Black Lives Matter rally in Austin in the summer of 2020.

Giving You a Voice

Was someone you love killed by police in the Austin area? Were you severely injured by police officers while participating in a peaceful protest that turned violent? If so, the attorneys at Hendler Flores Law can help. Our lawyers care deeply about victims of police brutality. We act intentionally to hold law enforcement officers accountable for their actions that resulted in your injuries or your loss. We care about your story and want to help you tell it. You deserve justice. 

Setting a High Standard for Client Care

At HFL, every client matters. We take the time to get to know you, the details of your case, and the impact of your injury or loss on your life and your family. As your legal representatives, we are committed to understanding what you hope to get out of the legal process. Our goal is to come up with a legal strategy that is customized based on your needs. 

You likely want compensation for your injuries or loss. You probably want the police officers punished for their crimes. You also might want to help prevent this type of tragedy from happening to other families. Whatever the most important issues are for you, we will take the time to listen to your wishes, and then we will work tirelessly to represent you and your family.

Police brutality cases are complicated, and our lawyers will take the time to help you understand your legal rights in simple terms. We will explain the differences between civil cases and criminal cases, and help you understand the complex challenge of officer qualified immunity (a legal precedent that protects police officers from being held personally responsible for their actions).

Featured Client Story

Widow Left to Provide for 3 Daughters After Losing Husband to Mesothelioma

Hendler Flores Law represented the family of a decorated Navy Veteran who was exposed to asbestos while serving on a warship in Vietnam. He later died from mesothelioma, leaving his wife to provide for their 3 young daughters. Scott Hendler and co-counsel Michael Brickman negotiated several settlements with asbestos companies for the family, and also won a $13.8 million verdict against Owens Corning Fiberglas, a company that made asbestos pipe covering for Navy vessels. The compensation recovered was more than enough to secure the financial future of the family.

$13.8 Million Jury Verdict in Mesothelioma Death
Financial Security for the Family

Thousands of Central American Banana Workers Develop Sterility from Toxic Pesticide

For more than 3 decades, Scott Hendler has been seeking justice for thousands of Central American banana plantation workers who developed sterility and other severe illnesses after being exposed to a dangerous chemical called DBCP. Even though the chemical had been banned in the US, several US-based multinational corporations continued to use it at their international plantations. Despite numerous legal obstacles and devious tactics by defendants, Scott continues to fight for these victims' rights.

Precedent-Setting US Supreme Court Victory
One of the Most Litigated Toxic Tort Cases in History

New Mexico Family Loses 2 Teenage Daughters in Collision With Drunk Driver

Hendler Flores Law and co-counsel represented a Navajo family that was involved in a catastrophic collision with an impaired driver. The driver had been drinking for several hours at 2 different establishments before getting behind the wheel. Two teenage girls were killed in the crash, which also left other family members critically injured. Our litigation team filed several lawsuits on behalf of the family, recovering a total of $30 million in compensation and securing seed money for the establishment of a charity foundation in honor of the family.

$30 Million Recovery in Impaired Driving Case
