Established legal precedent for victims who played on contaminated ball field.

In 1994, Mr. Anthony established legal precedent in New Jersey by certifying the first toxic exposure personal injury and medical monitoring class action (Russo v. Allied Signal). As a result of the settlement, a 30-year medical monitoring program was established for people who played on a chromium contaminated ball field in Jersey City, NJ.

Pompton Lakes community suit settles for $38.5 million against DuPont.

In 1997, Mr. Anthony received the Association of Trial Lawyers of America’s Outstanding Achievement Award for his work on behalf of 427 Pompton Lakes residents in an environmental contamination suit against DuPont that resulted in a $38.5 million settlement (Armona v. DuPont).

Represented over 38,000 victims of environmental contamination in class actions upheld on appeal.

Mr. Anthony has achieved numerous successful environmental and chemical exposure cases with highly favorable settlements for his clients. In addition to personal injury cases including cancer, and medical monitoring cases, he certified two property damage class actions in Tonawanda, NY and West Caldwell, NJ both which were upheld on appeal, representing over ten thousand homes and over 38,000 victims.

Millions in recoveries for injuries including leukemia and blood cancers for mechanics, auto mechanics, painters, gasoline workers, ship workers and other professionals around the country using products containing Benzene.

Millions in recoveries for individuals with Mesothelioma from exposure to Asbestos, including electricians, auto mechanics, construction workers, plumbers, insulators, talc users and 911 rescue workers.

Millions in recoveries for personal injuries from motor vehicle accidents and negligence cases, including recent $3.5 million settlement for a motor vehicle accident victim’s for pain and suffering.
